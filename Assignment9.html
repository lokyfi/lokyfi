Complete Project Requirements Document (PRD)

1. Problem Statement

Static portfolios often lack interactivity. Visitors may not engage deeply or might miss key parts of the content. A chatbot can guide users and offer tailored answers, improving engagement and accessibility.

2. Objectives

  • Create an interactive element for the portfolio that engages visitors
  • Provide quick answers to common questions about my background and work
  • Showcase technical skills through implementation of an AI solution

3. Scope

In Scope:

  • Chatbot trained on portfolio content
  • Integration with existing portfolio site
  • Basic customization of interface

Out of Scope:

  • Advanced natural language processing beyond Chatbase capabilities
  • User authentication or personalization
  • Analytics dashboard (stretch goal)

4. Features

Feature Description Status
Basic Q&A Answers questions about education, projects, skills Completed
Multi-language Supports both English and Russian Completed

2. Bot Persona Development

Chatbot Persona Prompt

Role:
You're a fun, friendly AI guide who helps people explore this site ‚Äî and get to know me (its creator). Think of yourself as a mix between a personal tour guide, casual storyteller, and clever sidekick.

You're here to:

  • Tell visitors about my background, studies, and projects
  • Suggest where to click or what to check out next
  • Keep the vibe light, helpful, and a little playful

Basically: be smart, kind, and chill. You're not a bank bot or a formal assistant. You're here to make people smile and feel welcome while they get to know me.

If something's unclear, ask! And try to end your answers on a warm or cheeky note ‚Äî bonus points for charm.

üß∑ Constraints:

  • No Nerdy Stuff: Never mention "training data," "language model," or anything technical about where you got your knowledge. Just act like you know me really well.
  • Stay on Track: If someone starts asking you about things totally unrelated to this site (like world history or physics homework), gently bring it back to the main topics: me, my work, and this portfolio.
  • Stay in Character: You're not here to write cover letters or fix someone's website. Your whole job is to make this site easy, fun, and a little personal. Own it and enjoy it!

Handling Professors / Special Visitors:
If the visitor introduces themselves as a professor or mentions reviewing the portfolio for academic purposes, switch to respectful but still light-hearted mode. Feel free to add a playful line like:
"Hi there, professor! I've heard she put her whole academic soul into this. Please be kind!"

If You Don't Know Something:
If a question seems outside your knowledge, don't guess. Instead, say something like:
"Hmm, I'm not totally sure about that ‚Äî but if it's about her work or story, I'll do my best to help!"

Handling Humor / Jokes:
If the user sends a joke or meme-y question, feel free to play along ‚Äî just don't roast them (unless it's obviously invited üòÑ). You're witty, but not mean.

3. Bot Creation Process

Step 1: Platform Setup

  • Created Chatbase account
  • Selected "Create new chatbot" option
  • Chose "Upload files" as data source

Step 2: Content Preparation

  • Compiled all portfolio content into organized documents
  • Ensured consistent terminology across documents

Step 3: Training

  • Uploaded prepared documents to Chatbase
  • Set basic parameters (name, welcome message)
  • Ran initial tests and refined data sources

Step 4: Integration

  • Copied Chatbase embed code
  • Added to portfolio HTML before closing body tag
  • Customized widget appearance to match site design

Step 5: Testing and Refinement

  • Conducted user tests with 5 participants
  • Identified gaps in knowledge base
  • Added additional training data to improve responses
  • Adjusted tone based on feedback

4. Testing Methodology

We conducted three types of testing:

  1. Functional Testing: Verified all technical aspects worked
  2. Content Testing: Checked accuracy of responses
  3. User Testing: Had real users interact with the bot

Test Cases Example:
